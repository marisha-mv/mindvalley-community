01 — The opportunity

Build the world's first AI Guild — not a Mastery upsell

The wrong question is "what can we sell our Mastery students?" — that's a small pool and a small ceiling. The right question is "how do we build a $30M product?" That changes everything. We're not building a community for the Mindvalley base. We're building the room that every serious person in AI wants to be in — globally — the way Chief built it for women leaders.

The paid peer-network model is proven at scale: Chief ($5,800/yr, 120k members, $1.1B valuation), Vistage ($13k/yr, $201M revenue), EO ($2,470/yr, 18k members). They charge $5–25k a year and the cheapest is the floor. Nobody owns the AI niche yet. We can.

The product isn't content. The product is people. You don't join because Mindvalley made it — you join because the people you follow and want to learn from are inside.

The core bet

Caliber over quantity. We'd rather grow slowly to 5,000 exceptional members with a real bar, than dump everyone in and have a dead room. The quality of who's in the room is what people pay for and what makes them stay.

02 — What the AI Guild is

A network on the frontier, not a course

A closed, application-only network of people pursuing excellence with AI. You don't enrol — you get in. Its job is to keep you on top of what's changing, connect you with people ahead of you, and get you the tools and access to move faster.

◇

A light, living curriculum

Not a fixed syllabus — a stream of what's trending, what's new, what's hot. The "curriculum" is staying current as AI changes weekly.
✦

Application-only, real guardrails

Everyone applies. Like Chief: being qualified isn't enough to get in. A concierge vets every applicant; we reject easily.
△

Annual subscription

Recurring, evergreen, start anytime. Built for retention and predictable revenue.
❍

AI + human run

An AI Guild Master plus a community manager keep the room alive every day, so it never goes quiet.

How we're different from Chief

Chief connects women leaders so they have an outlet — they don't share results, revenue or data; it's private peer support. We do the opposite: we drive and show results. We help you build your first app, grow your audience, launch a company — and we turn those wins into case studies that prove the room works. Our promise is concrete: up-to-date information, the latest tools, and the latest people in the game.

03 — Who gets in

Builders, not noobs

Founders, operators and creators using AI substantially — people with a business, a product, or real momentum. Doing AI Mastery doesn't get you in. The question is: did you perform, did you learn, are you actually building? The Guild has a bar, and the bar is the point.

The application — everyone applies, free

Applying is free; the gate is caliber, not a fee. The flow mirrors Chief: apply → a concierge reviews the form → a short call → accepted or rejected. The friction is deliberate — it's what makes the badge worth $5,000.

Fast-track — Mastery grads
  • Still apply — but a fast-tracked form
  • Approved in ~24 hours if they qualify
  • Must have performed in Mastery, not just enrolled
  • Earns a first-year discount (see §7)
Full application — everyone else
  • Proper application + concierge call
  • Judged on real expertise & output
  • Rejection is common, by design
  • Rejected? Offered a seat at the next Mastery to qualify later

Turning a rejection into a Mastery invitation does two things: it softens the "no," and it makes Mastery the on-ramp to the Guild — so the Guild pulls people into Mastery, not just out of it.

04 — The network effect

The real product is the people in the room

A guild with no one in it is worthless — the first thousand people are everything. So before we sell a single membership, we build the talent layer: the faces people join to be near. We're solving a marketing problem, and influence is how we solve it.

~150 up-and-coming AI influencers the founding faces

Not mega-names (they won't contribute, and they're too big to feel exclusive). Micro-influencers — 10k–50k followers — who teach AI on Instagram/TikTok, are hustling, and want to grow. We bring them in as founding members and the faces of the Guild, pay them commission on members they bring (e.g. a share of each sale), pay them to run workshops/calls, and give them a stage at the AI Summit. They expose the Guild to their combined audiences; their followers join to be in the room with them. That's the engine.

B2B companies logos + seats

Reach out to companies (including via the company emails of people who join) with a launch price — e.g. $1,000/seat instead of $5,000 — to land recognizable logos on the page early. A few marquee company names make the Guild credible to everyone else.

The affiliate / creator base distribution we already have

We have ~29,000 affiliate applications on Impact plus our creator lists. Nurtured and educated (which offers convert, how to promote), they become a standing distribution arm for the Guild launch and beyond.

Why this comes first

In a sales call, the first question is "how many people are in your Guild — and who?" You can't answer "we're about to launch." You answer with names and faces. So we build the founding talent layer before we open the doors.

05 — What's inside

The value stack

Pay $5,000 and the value has to be obvious and stacked. Here's what a member gets.

Weekly Guild Master sessions stay current

The Guild Master runs frequent, practical sessions on what's new and hot this week — how to use a tool, automate a workflow, solve a real problem (e.g. "edit all your videos with AI," "automate X with Claude"). Plus tool discovery: what's worth it, what isn't.

Monthly expert session the bigger picture

One marquee expert a month who's achieved something drastic with AI, teaching the larger play — not just tactics. Drawn from our pool of influencers and operators.

Mastermind pods & project groups solve real problems together

Member-led pods around specific AI problems — vibe-coding / Claude Code, social-media growth-hacking, automating work, building an app. Smaller rooms where people actually move their projects forward.

The AI tools wall $50k of value for $5k

A wall of AI tools where the Guild is an affiliate and members get 20–30% discounts. Spend $5,000 on the Guild, save $50,000 on tools — the math sells itself, and it's a real revenue line for us.

Product demo & launch days the frontier comes to you

Vetted companies launch and demo new AI products inside the Guild (e.g. the next Loom). Members see the newest tools first; companies pay us for the demo day (e.g. ~$50k) and we can affiliate the tool. Members stay on the bleeding edge; we monetize the access.

Talent & connection matching build your AI team

Launching an AI company? Find your CPO, your Chief of AI, the developers and designers you need — matched by your goals. The Guild recommends who to meet and brokers the introduction, so the network produces tangible outcomes.

Proof harvesting results, not vanity

When a member gets a real result with AI — business grows, audience grows, app ships, company launches — we capture it as a case study showing the Guild drove it. This is our edge over Chief: we prove outcomes.

07 — The offer

The offer & pricing

The industry charges $5–25k a year; $5k is the floor. We price at the floor of premium — high enough to signal caliber, accessible enough to scale. One Guild, one price. We make this one work before we build anything else.

  • Weekly Guild Master sessions + monthly expert
  • Mastermind pods & project groups
  • AI tools wall (20–30% discounts)
  • Demo days, talent matching, AI Guild Master
Mastery fast-track
$2,500/yr · first year only

The 50% Mastery discount applies to the first year only — renewals are full price. It incentivizes doing (and performing in) Mastery without permanently discounting the Guild, and the product has to be good enough that they happily renew at $5,000.

Beyond subscription, the Guild earns from product demo days (~$50k each), the AI tools affiliate wall, and B2B seat deals — real revenue lines on top of membership.

08 — The numbers

First 1,000 members, then caliber at scale

A premium room still needs critical mass — 200 people where 70% don't show up is a dead Guild. We aim for a minimum of ~1,000 active, high-caliber members at launch, then grow the bar, not just the count.

Year-one note

09 — How we launch

Internal first, external second

Two phases. Internal is easier and comes first — our own list. External is harder and follows — influencers, brands and affiliates. We can launch the offer and collect applications months before the Guild actually opens.

Internal launch — to everyone, not just Mastery

Expose the offer to the whole list, not only Mastery students — educate them on what it is, then ask them to apply. The application asks if they're a Mastery grad (→ fast-track) or not (→ full application + possible Mastery invitation if rejected). This also surfaces the hidden gems: plenty of people on our list are more advanced than Mastery grads — they just never bought Mastery because they thought it was basic.

The marketing — direct, not a boring webinar

Do
  • A short, cool trailer / product video showing the bigger picture
  • A landing page that lays out the features (it's self-explanatory)
  • Free application straight off the page
  • ~15 emails + WhatsApp + news-feed, staggered across the month
  • A concierge who personally reviews forms and runs the calls
Don't
  • A long vision-led VSL (people get turned off)
  • A heavy webinar that explains everything
  • An application fee (free to apply — gate on caliber)
  • Auto-accept anyone, including Mastery grads
  • Trickle people in with no scarcity

The timeline

JulyBuild the talent layer + page/trailer. Start ~15 staggered application emails. Begin expert/influencer outreach.
AugustOpen applications. Concierge reviews + booked calls begin. B2B + influencer outreach in full.
SeptemberKeep promoting; staggered acceptance calls; confirm the founding cohort. External hype builds.
OctoberGuild opens — members get access. External launch moment around the live room.

External launch

Once the internal cohort and founding faces are locked, go wide: ~150 micro-influencers activating their audiences as affiliates, ~50 brands, and the B2B + Impact affiliate base. By then we can say "here's who's already inside" — which is what makes the external pitch land.

10 — Where this goes next

One Guild now. A platform later.

We make the AI Guild excellent first — if it only makes a million, there's no point. Once it works, the bigger picture opens up.

▣

A platform of guilds

Eventually anyone on FinerMinds could create their own guild — a plumbing guild, an authorship guild — vetted by application. Mindvalley becomes the rails.
◈

Brand vs. box

Keep the language clean: "Guild" is the premium, $5,000, vetted brand offering. Generic member-created spaces are "communities." Don't blur the two.
✦

More Mindvalley guilds

Social media, authorship, manifesting — later. AI is the easiest and the proof-of-concept; the rest follow once one is a clear success.
◐

In-person, globally

Chief is US-only; we can run meetups anywhere. In-person gatherings are a longer-term edge once the room is full.
