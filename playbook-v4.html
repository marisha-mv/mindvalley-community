01 — The strategy

Community is how we retain

Members don't buy the community. They buy a pathway — the quests, the meditations, and the community together. Community is one part of that, and its job is clear: community is our answer to retention. It's what makes members stay.

It does that four ways. It makes people feel seen. It collects data — what members care about, what they want to learn, the questions they're asking. The community manager guides — answering those questions and pointing members to the right quests, summits and products. And it's marketing: today we lean on email to sell the next offer; the community becomes the channel that does it instead.

Retention, data, guidance, and a marketing channel — all in one room. That's why getting it right matters.

Nurture members well here and they stay, they go deeper into the quests, and they buy what comes next — without an email blast doing the work.

02 — The operator mindset

Run it like it's yours

This role is for autonomous operators. You are not a moderator waiting to be told what to post. You own your community's agenda, curriculum and calendar, and you run it like a founder runs a product.

That means you set the plan, you know what's coming, you build the curriculum, you book the people, and you hold yourself to the numbers. The community succeeds or fails on your initiative — and you're rewarded on the outcome.

04 — How you show up

Present, warm, and unmistakably you

You are the face of the community. The energy you bring is the energy members mirror back. Show up daily, in voice and in presence — a quiet community is a dying one.

Your voice & energy

Warm, direct, encouraging. You celebrate loudly, you ask real questions, you never talk down. You embody your pathway — the Longevity manager lives the protocols; the Founders manager thinks like a builder. Members should be able to feel which community they're in from the tone alone.

And it's not just the words — it's the energy behind them. There has to be a feel in the room that makes people want to be part of the conversation. Your language should carry that energy: alive, magnetic, the kind people lean into. A flat, functional tone empties a room as fast as silence does.

Be visible & responsive

Reply, react, and acknowledge fast. A member who posts a win and hears nothing back has just learned the room is empty. Your fingerprints should be all over the feed every day.

On FinerMinds

Live in the activity feed daily — react and reply so members see you. Use 1:1 and group chat for the personal touches that don't belong in public. Keep your own profile complete and human — photo, bio, interests — you set the standard members follow.

05 — How you dress & present

Show up on camera like the brand

The marquee live calls are author-led (see §8) — but you'll still be on camera plenty: welcomes, pod sessions, casual check-ins. Whenever you appear, you represent Mindvalley, and members judge the community by how you present. Hold a premium bar.

Do
  • Dress clean, intentional and brand-aligned — elevated-casual, polished, never sloppy
  • Frame at eye level with a tidy, on-brand background
  • Light your face well; soft, front-facing light
  • Test audio — clear sound matters more than camera
  • Bring high, warm energy from the first second
Don't
  • Roll on camera in loungewear or straight out of bed
  • Sit backlit, in shadow, or against clutter
  • Use a tinny mic or a noisy room
  • Show up flat, distracted, or low-energy
  • Treat any on-camera moment as casual — it's a brand moment

The standard is simple: look like someone members would pay to learn from. Because they do.

06 — How you nurture

The relationship work

Nurture is the job. It's the daily, unglamorous work of making people feel seen, helping them grow, and pointing them to what's next. The moves:

1 · Welcome everyone, personally

Every new member gets greeted by name and introduced to the community. First touch decides whether they feel seen — make it human, never automated.

2 · Make people feel seen

Shout out wins. Reply to the quiet ones. Notice when someone's gone silent and reach out. If a member could vanish unnoticed, the system failed.

3 · Spark connection between members

Belonging isn't just member-to-manager — it's member-to-member. Introduce people with shared goals; create reasons for them to meet.

4 · Run the transformation feed

Prompt members to post their journey out loud, so the feed fills with proof and momentum. When one person shares a win, others get inspired, do more, and share back. It compounds.

How did week 1 go? What are you working on? Your biggest result this month? What's your goal right now? Share your transformation

5 · Point them to the next thing

Nurture isn't only emotional — it's directional. Part of the job is discovery: introduce members to the right lessons, quests, products and summits. If this week's conversation is about a topic, push them to the quest lesson that goes deeper. Mindvalley's content only transforms people if they find it — you point the way.

6 · Highlight transformations to inspire

Spotlight real member transformations — they're the most powerful thing in the room. If a Longevity member went from 101kg to running a Hyrox, tell that story so the next person sees what's possible and thinks "I could do that too." Make these spotlights a regular feature, not an accident.

On FinerMinds

Welcome new members through chat and a feed post. Use "Members to Meet" — its smart recommendations by shared interests — to spark introductions, especially in Relationships & Community. Lean on member profiles, interests and tagging to personalise outreach, and run cohort conversation in group chat and Level Chat tied to progression.

08 — The live call

The live call is the author's stage

To be confirmed — needs Vishen's approval

Vishen has reservations about adding calls, so this whole section is provisional until he signs off. Two open questions: whether we run live calls at all, and — if membership already includes recorded content plus community — whether the live-call experience belongs in Academy rather than membership.

If we run them: live calls are hosted by people with real clout — Vishen or major authors — not the community manager. Author-led calls are what drive engagement; a marquee name is the draw. Your job isn't to host. It's to produce and amplify. The point of every call is to drive consumption, discovery and engagement — pulling members into the content.

Keep them short and purposeful

Run a call roughly every two weeks, and keep them short — 30 minutes, punchy, not a sprawling hour. We don't want to compete with Mastery; pile on too many long calls and members attend nothing. Every call has a topic set in advance, and it's one of three things: a discussion on a topic, a debrief of the week's homework and what people produced, or an introduction to a new author.

Your job around the call

Line the author up against the calendar, fill the room (warm members up beforehand and send the invites), facilitate live (run the chat, wrangle the Q&A, hold the energy), and follow up after — post the replay and prompt members to share what they took from it. You make the call land; the author carries the stage.

Be calendar-aware — seed before the sale

Line up author calls ahead of what's selling. If a summit is coming, get its authors in front of members early so they arrive primed. Example: the Singapore Accelerator with Daniel Priestley — get him on a call early so members know him and the material, and when the accelerator opens, they already want in.

On FinerMinds

Schedule the author's call through Events, fill it with personal broadcast invites, and post the replay to the document library so members who missed it still get the value.

09 — The 90-day content plan

A guided 90-day journey, one tip at a time

Every pathway runs on a 90-day content plan — a steady stream of small, genuinely useful tips, not generic posts. From the day a member joins, they should feel they're on a guided journey, getting something each day that makes them a little better.

Join Exponential Founders and you get a daily tip that makes you a sharper entrepreneur. Join Longevity and you get a daily move toward more energy and a longer life. The pathway stops being a place you visit and becomes a journey you're walking.

Where the tips come from

Gareth builds the 90-day journey for each pathway, generated with AI from the full transcript of each program — drawn straight from the authors' own teaching, so every tip is specific and useful, never filler. One program becomes ninety days of community fuel. Your job is simple: post it one tip at a time, on a steady cadence.

Built for engagement, not broadcast

A tip on its own is just a post. Every tip carries a specific ask — do this, then share it — exactly like the manifesting posts that asked members to share a photo of their journal page. The goal isn't to inform; it's to get members acting and posting, so each tip becomes a thread full of proof.

How you run it

The 90-day plan is your content backbone — no blank page, ever. You post the daily tip, then layer your polls and the weekly live call on top, and use each tip's prompt to keep the feed alive. The plan carries the rhythm; you bring the human touch.

On FinerMinds

The AI draws its tips from the program transcripts held in the document library. Post each day's tip to the feed, tag it to the pathway, and let the engagement prompt drive the comments — so the day's tip becomes the day's thread.

10 — The tech layer: build your AI

Leverage AI, build your own dashboard

Gareth's 90-day journey is the baseline — but it's only 90 days, and it's the same for everyone. The real leverage is the AI you build for yourself: a dashboard wired into the brain of every program transcript and your library of member testimonials, that tells you what to say, what to ask, and what to promote.

What your AI does for you

It hands you content to post, questions to raise, and the lessons worth promoting this week — drawn from the transcripts and the real results members are reporting. You stop staring at a blank page and start working from a feed of ideas tuned to your community and the calendar.

How it works in practice

You ask it the way you'd ask a strategist. "The Manifesting Summit is two months out — what should I be doing now?" It answers: start introducing the speakers, drop value bombs from their work, get people doing the manifesting quest, run a "who wants to do this quest with me this week?" challenge. The ideas come from the AI; you bring the human energy to them. Every community manager should be building their own.

On FinerMinds

Point your AI at the program transcripts and replays in the document library, the testimonials you've collected, and the analytics on what's landing — then let it surface what to post, what to promote, and when, against the launch calendar.

11 — The FinerMinds toolkit

Every feature, mapped to the job

The platform gives you everything you need to run all of the above. Here's not just what each feature is for, but exactly how you use it day to day.

Branded community spaces the home for each community

You're Admin, so you set the tone the moment someone walks in. Keep the banner and pinned welcome post current, pin this week's focus and the day's tip so newcomers land in motion, and keep one clean space per pathway so each community's identity stays distinct. What you pin and feature is what members see first — use that real estate deliberately.

Sub-groups the smaller rooms

Spin up a sub-group for every member-started challenge and every niche pod (runners, ecom), and a cohort group so people who joined the same month move through it together. Never launch one empty — seed each new pod with a starter prompt and a pod host, so the first member to arrive finds a conversation already going.

Activity feed the transformation wall

Post the day's tip with its share prompt, then reshare member wins to the top so the feed reads as mostly members, not you. Run polls, drop "share your week 1" prompts, and spotlight one transformation story a week. React fast to every post — a win that gets no response teaches a member the room is empty.

1:1, group & Level Chat the personal layer

DM every new member a real, personal welcome within their first day. Check in 1:1 with anyone who's gone quiet before they drift. Run cohort and pod conversation in group chat, and use Level Chat to reward progression with a higher-tier room. Rule of thumb: vulnerable and personal goes in chat, celebratory goes in the feed.

Events the live calls (pending approval)

Schedule the author's call, set its topic in advance, and fill the room with personal-broadcast invites and a day-of reminder. Afterward, post the replay to the library and a "what was your one takeaway?" prompt to keep the conversation going. (Subject to Vishen's sign-off on calls — see §8.)

Member profiles & "Members to Meet" context + connection

Read a new member's profile before you welcome them, so you can reference their actual goal — it's the difference between "welcome!" and "welcome, saw you're training for a marathon." Use "Members to Meet" to introduce two members with shared goals every week, and push newcomers to complete their profile and set a goal in their first session — that's how both you and the AI know who they are.

Email + bulk CSV invitations onboarding & activation

Invite new members in waves so each cohort starts together rather than trickling in alone. Watch invite-status to catch the people who joined but never activated, and reach out personally — that list is your highest-leverage save. For the free entrepreneurship community, this is where you run the apply-and-refer flow.

Document library + AI search the content store & the AI's brain

House the 90-day journey, worksheets and call replays here, and tag everything to pathway and topic so members — and your AI — can actually find it. When a conversation calls for it, point a member to the exact lesson. This library is also the source your own AI dashboard (§10) pulls from to tell you what to promote.

Stats & analytics your scoreboard

Check it weekly: who's active, who's gone quiet, what's landing. Watch your week-one activation, engagement frequency and retention uplift, and let the numbers tell you which members to rescue and which content to do more of. Pull retention and revenue figures through the API into your own dashboard.

Moderation & member management the trust layer

Remove spam fast, set and enforce the norms, and protect the vulnerable sharing that makes the whole thing work — a safe room is the precondition for everyone else opening up. Promote your most generous members into pod hosts; the people who already help others are your best leaders.

12 — What's expected of you

The standards you're held to

Autonomy comes with a quality bar. These are non-negotiable:

The standard
  • Daily presence in the feed — react, reply, acknowledge
  • Every new member welcomed personally
  • A poll/survey at least every two weeks
  • An author-led live call roughly every two weeks, if approved — produced and filled by you
  • Testimonials harvested continuously
  • A clean, safe, on-brand space — moderated actively
Falling short
  • Silent feeds and unanswered members
  • Generic, copy-paste welcomes
  • Author calls booked last-minute, or a half-empty room
  • Reacting to the launch calendar instead of seeding ahead
  • Letting the space go quiet between events
  • Off-brand presentation on camera

14 — What good looks like

Operating principles

  • Run it like it's yours. Own your agenda, curriculum and calendar. Don't wait to be directed.
  • Always be laddering. Every interaction nudges toward the next quest, the next call, or the next product.
  • Make the invisible visible. Surface members' growth so they can feel themselves changing.
  • Seed ahead of the calendar. Warm members for what's coming — never react after it's launched.
  • Every member feels seen. If someone could vanish without being noticed, you missed the job.
  • Hold the premium bar. How you show up — in voice, on camera, in the feed — is the value members feel.
